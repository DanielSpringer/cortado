About the ensemble

Close enough to feel human, refined enough to suit a contemporary brand.

Cortado con Brio keeps the performance language elegant and direct. The repertoire is rooted in the classical canon, but the presentation is designed to resonate in modern venues and digital campaigns.

The visual identity leans into shadow, brass light, and partial glimpses of musicians at work, so the page feels alive without becoming visually noisy.

The ensemble traces its public chamber-music language to the Vienna Chamber Players, founded in 2017 by violinist Huiseung Yoo and clarinetist Helmut Hödl.

We favor intimacy over spectacle, clarity over ornament, and resonance over noise.

The Vienna Chamber Players lineup behind Cortado con Brio.

Mini profiles based on the published Vienna Chamber Players lineup on Helmut Hödl's project page. Archived event lineups vary, so this section uses the directly listed ensemble members as the baseline.

Violin

Huiseung Yoo

Founding violinist of Vienna Chamber Players and listed by SOVOP as 1. Violin / First Chair at the Volksoper orchestra.

Violin

Dejana Goločevac

Second violin voice in the Vienna Chamber Players lineup, shaping the ensemble's compact string sound and chamber balance.

Viola

Peter Sagaischek

Violist of Vienna Chamber Players, anchoring the inner register and the ensemble's warm Viennese chamber color.

Violoncello

Benedikt Hellsberg

Cellist in the published Vienna Chamber Players lineup, providing the lyrical bass foundation for string-led programs.

Double bass

Tomáš Petőcz

Double bassist in the ensemble lineup, extending the group toward a flexible chamber-orchestral sonority.

Clarinet

Helmut Hödl

Co-founder, clarinetist, arranger, and composer for Vienna Chamber Players, connecting classical repertoire with new arrangements.

Sources: Helmut Hödl / Vienna Chamber Players and SOVOP profiles for Huiseung Yoo , Dejana Goločevac , Peter Sagaischek , Benedikt Hellsberg , Tomáš Petőcz , and Helmut Hödl .
